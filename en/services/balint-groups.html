What are Balint Groups? How do Balint Groups work?

What are Balint Groups?

Balint groups are a form of professional group work designed for people working in healthcare, mental health, and other helping professions. They create a safe and supportive environment where professionals can explore relationships, emotional responses, and professional difficulties encountered in their work.

The main purpose of Balint groups is to develop a deeper understanding of the professional–client relationship. During the group process, participants can view their professional experiences from new perspectives, better understand their own responses, and develop professional self-awareness.

How do Balint Groups work?

Balint groups are conducted in small groups led by an experienced facilitator. During each meeting, one participant presents a real professional case, which is then explored by the group.

The discussion focuses not on diagnosis or treatment strategy, but on the relationship between the professional and the client, the emotions that arise, and the experience formed during the work. This process supports professional self-understanding, empathy, and more reflective practice.

Balint groups can be useful for psychologists, psychotherapists, psychiatrists, physicians, nurses, social workers, and other professionals whose work involves ongoing contact with people. They are particularly valuable when dealing with professional pressure, complex clinical cases, emotional difficulties, and the prevention of burnout.

Balint groups contribute to professional development, self-reflection, and improvement in the quality of the professional–client relationship. They create an opportunity to learn not only from one’s own experience, but also from the experiences of other group members.
