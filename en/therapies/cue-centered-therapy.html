What is Cue-Centered Therapy (CCT)?

Cue-Centered Therapy (CCT) is an evidence-based psychotherapeutic approach developed for children and adolescents who have experienced traumatic or repeated stressful events. It helps them understand how these experiences continue to affect emotions, thoughts, behavior, and daily life, while developing more effective coping skills.

The approach is based on the idea that certain internal or external cues can activate traumatic memories and cause strong emotional or physical responses, even when the original danger is no longer present. Therapy helps identify these cues and develop safer, more controlled responses.

How does Cue-Centered Therapy work?

During therapy, the therapist and client explore the situations, thoughts, emotions, and physical sensations that activate traumatic experiences. The client gradually learns to identify these cues, understand their effects, and use effective emotional self-regulation strategies.

The therapy combines psychoeducation, emotional regulation skills, safe processing of traumatic experiences, identifying personal strengths, and increasing awareness. The work is adapted to each person’s individual needs and level of readiness.

Cue-Centered Therapy is used with children and adolescents affected by trauma, violence, loss, chronic stress, anxiety, or symptoms of post-traumatic stress disorder. Its principles may also be applied when working with adults.

The effectiveness of Cue-Centered Therapy has been supported by scientific research, particularly in the treatment of childhood and adolescent trauma.
